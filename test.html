Mutual Fund

A mutual fund is a professionally managed investment fund that pools money from many investors to purchase securities. These investors may be retail or institutional in nature.

Mutual funds provide diversification, professional management, and liquidity. However, investors need to consider associated risks and expenses.

Primary structures of mutual funds include open-ended funds and closed-ended funds.

Debt Funds

A debt fund invests primarily in bonds or debt securities, focusing on income generation and capital preservation.

Equity Funds

An equity mutual fund invests in equities and related securities, aiming for long-term capital appreciation.

Hybrid Funds

Hybrid funds invest in a mix of equities and fixed-income securities, balancing growth and stability.

Solution Oriented Funds

Designed for specific financial goals like retirement or education, often with a lock-in period.

Other Funds

Includes categories like Index Funds and ETFs, each targeting specific investment objectives.
